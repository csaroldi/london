Before You Go

The Essentials

Everything you need to know before leaving the hotel.

Use contactless or Apple Pay — never buy a paper ticketTap in and out on any yellow reader. Works on Tube, buses, Overground, and Elizabeth line.
Piccadilly line — direct to Heathrow from Earl's Court45 minutes, no changes. Take it over any cab unless you have a mountain of luggage.
Bus route 11 — the best free tour in LondonFulham to Liverpool Street via Westminster, Trafalgar Square, St Paul's. Upper deck, same tap.
Boris Bikes (Santander Cycles) are everywhereDownload TfL app. First 30 min essentially free. Perfect for Hyde Park and South Bank.
Black cabs are excellent — but priceyDrivers pass The Knowledge — years memorising every street. Worth it at least once for the conversation.
Currency is Pound Sterling (£) — not EurosThe UK is not in the EU. Do not try to pay with Euros — no one will accept them.
London is almost cashless — card is kingKeep £20–40 cash for emergencies but you may never need it.
Tipping: 10–12.5% in restaurantsMost add a service charge automatically. Pubs: no tip expected for bar drinks.
Major museums are completely freeBritish Museum, Natural History Museum, V&A, National Gallery, Tate Modern — all free.
It will probably rain. Plan accordingly.12 rainy days per month year-round. Never trust the morning forecast. Bring a compact umbrella.
Layers, always — even in summerSummer highs reach 20–24°C but drop to 14°C in the evening. Always dress in layers.
Comfortable shoes are non-negotiableYou will do 15,000 steps without noticing. Cobblestones at Shad Thames are unforgiving.
Get an eSIM before you flyAiralo sells UK eSIMs from around £5 for 1GB. Install on the plane, works on landing.
Essential apps before the tripTfL Go, Citymapper, Google Maps offline (download SW London area), Uber.
999— UK emergency services. Police, ambulance, fire. Life-threatening emergencies only.
111— NHS non-emergency medical helpline. Advice, minor injuries. Available 24/7.
101— Non-emergency police. Lost property, minor incidents.
Brazilian Consulate: +44 20 7747 4500— 14–16 Cockspur St, SW1Y 5BL. Near Trafalgar Square.

Insider Tips

Good to Know

The things the guidebooks either miss or refuse to say.

You order at the bar — no table service in a traditional pubWalk up, wait your turn, order and pay. Leaving money on the table does not work.
Do not shout or wave to get the bartender's attentionMake eye contact and wait. Waving money or clicking fingers is rude. Patience is the system.
Buying rounds — it is a social contractAccept the first round offered. Never buy just your own when others are buying rounds — it breaks the code.
Last orders bell — you have minutes when it ringsThe bell is your cue to get one more round — not to leave. Finish your drinks before you go.
Stand on the right on escalators. Always.The left is for walking. Blocking it with luggage generates genuine hostility. The most important rule in London.
Do not make eye contact on the TubeLondoners in the Tube are in their own world. Read something. Look at your phone. It is the custom.
Let people off before you get onStand aside, let everyone off, then board. Boarding while people exit earns genuine disgust.
Mind the Gap — the gaps are genuinely realOn curved platforms the gap can break an ankle. The announcement is not decorative.
London is enormous — far bigger than it looks on a mapTwo adjacent Tube stops can be 40 min on foot. The Tube map distorts distances massively.
Everything costs more than you expectA pint is £6–8. A coffee is £4–5. Dinner for two without wine is easily £60–80. Budget generously.
Queuing is sacred — never jump a queueJoin at the back, always. Jumping generates quiet but unmistakable hostility. Non-negotiable.
Never call Tower Bridge London BridgeTower Bridge: towers and bascules, iconic. London Bridge: plain, flat, upstream. Every Londoner will notice.
Locals walk across bridges — tourists take the TubeWestminster to Tower Bridge via the South Bank takes an hour and passes everything. Best way to see London.
Locals eat outside whenever the sun appears — regardless of temperatureParks fill within minutes of sunshine appearing. Join them.
Locals avoid Borough Market on Saturday lunchtimeThursday–Friday: quieter traders, space to breathe, same food. Saturday lunchtime is wall-to-wall tourists.
Tate Modern Level 10 is free and has extraordinary viewsThe Blavatnik Building viewing level: free, panoramic views over the Thames, St Paul's and the City. No ticket.

A Few Words of English

British English has its own vocabulary. A few words will save you from confusion — and embarrassment.

Everyday Expressions

said constantly
Thank you / Goodbye / You're welcome — context decides which
Sorted
casual
Done / Arranged / No problem
Brilliant
enthusiastic
Excellent / Amazing / That's great
Lovely
very common
Great / Perfect — used to confirm almost anything
Pubs & Food
A pint of lager, please
at the bar
A standard beer. Lager = cold, light. Ale = darker, room temperature
Can I get the bill, please?
at a restaurant
The check. Say this — do not mime writing in the air
It's a bit pricey
classic understatement
It is extremely expensive. British understatement at its finest
