Before You Start

The Essentials

Everything you need to know before leaving the hotel.

Use contactless or Apple Pay — never buy a paper ticketJust tap in and out on any yellow reader. Works on the Tube, buses, Overground, and Elizabeth line. Cheaper than Oyster and zero faff.
The Piccadilly line goes direct to Heathrow from Earl's Court45 minutes, no changes. Take it over any cab unless you have a mountain of luggage.
Buses are slower but you see the cityGrab a seat on the upper deck of a double decker. Same tap-in system. Route 11 goes past Westminster, St Paul's, Bank — essentially a free sightseeing tour.
Boris Bikes (Santander Cycles) — available everywhereDownload the TfL app. The first 30 minutes of each hire is essentially free. Perfect for Hyde Park, South Bank, and the canal paths.
Uber works fine — black cabs are excellent but priceyBlack cab drivers must pass The Knowledge — years of memorising every London street. Worth it at least once for the conversation alone.
Currency is Pound Sterling (£) — not EurosThe UK is not in the EU. Do not try to pay with Euros — no one will accept them.
London is almost cashless — card is kingVirtually everything accepts Visa and Mastercard contactless. Keep £20–40 cash for emergencies but you may never need it.
Tipping in restaurants: 10–12.5% is the normMost restaurants add a 'discretionary service charge' to the bill automatically. You can remove it if you want to tip in cash instead. Pubs: no tip expected for drinks at the bar.
Museums: most major ones are completely freeBritish Museum, Natural History Museum, V&A, National Gallery, Tate Modern — all free. The city subsidises culture heavily. Book timed entry online to skip queues.
It will probably rain. Plan accordingly.London averages rain on about 12 days per month year-round. Never trust the morning forecast — the weather changes fast. Bring a compact umbrella or a light waterproof jacket.
Layers, always — even in summerSummer highs reach 20–24°C on good days but drop to 14°C in the evening. Spring and autumn: 8–16°C. Dress to peel layers off through the day.
Comfortable shoes are non-negotiableLondon is a walking city. You will do 15,000 steps without noticing. Cobblestones at Shad Thames and the City will punish fashionable footwear.
Get an eSIM before you flyAiralo sells UK data eSIMs from around £5 for 1GB. Install it on the plane. No queue at the airport, works the moment you land.
Essential apps to download before the tripTfL Go (live Tube maps), Citymapper (best navigation in London), Google Maps offline (download SW London area), Uber, and the NHS App just in case.
Free Wi-Fi is widely available but patchyAll Tube stations have Wi-Fi on the platform (not in the tunnels). Most cafés, museums and large shops offer free Wi-Fi — just ask.
999— UK emergency services. Police, ambulance, fire. Use for life-threatening emergencies only.
111— NHS non-emergency medical helpline. Advice, minor injuries, illness. Available 24/7.
101— Non-emergency police. Lost property, minor incidents. Not for urgent situations.
Lost Tube property: 0343 222 1234— TfL lost property office. Surprisingly good at reuniting things with their owners.
Brazilian Consulate: +44 20 7747 4500— 14-16 Cockspur St, SW1Y 5BL. Near Trafalgar Square.

The oldest of London's eight Royal Parks, originally a deer park for Henry VIII in 1532.

Pelicans have lived in the park since 1664. Their descendants are still fed daily at 2:30pm.

Westminster Abbey
▾ INFO
📜 History

Founded in 960 AD, this Gothic masterpiece has hosted every royal coronation since 1066.

✦ Did You Know

The oldest door in the UK — nearly 1,000 years old — is hidden inside. Most visitors walk past it.

Big Ben & Westminster Bridge
▾ INFO
📜 History

Big Ben is the name of the 13.7-tonne bell inside the Elizabeth Tower, not the tower itself.

✦ Did You Know

The clock's accuracy is maintained by adding or removing old penny coins from the pendulum.

London Eye
▾ INFO
📜 History

Opened in 2000 as a temporary millennium structure. At 135 metres, each of 32 capsules represents a London borough.

✦ Did You Know

A full rotation takes 30 minutes and the wheel never stops. On a clear day you can see Windsor Castle 40km away.

The Insider Version

Good to Know

The things the guidebooks either miss or refuse to say.

You order at the bar — no table service in a traditional pubWalk up, wait your turn, order and pay. Leaving money on the table does not work.
Do not shout to get the bartender's attentionMake eye contact and wait. Waving money or clicking fingers is rude. Patience is the system.
Buying rounds — it is a social contractAccept the first round offered. Never buy just your own when others are buying rounds — it breaks the code.
Last orders bell — you have minutes when it ringsThe bell is your cue to get one more round in — not to leave. Finish your drinks before you go.
Stand on the right on escalators. Always.The left is for walking. Blocking it with luggage generates genuine hostility. The most important rule in London.
Do not make eye contact on the TubeLondoners in the Tube are in their own world. Read something. Look at your phone. It is the custom.
Let people off before you get onStand aside, let everyone off, then board. Boarding while people exit earns genuine disgust.
Mind the Gap — the gaps are genuinely realOn curved platforms the gap can break an ankle. The announcement is not decorative.
London is enormous — far bigger than it looks on a mapTwo adjacent Tube stops can be 40 min on foot. The Tube map distorts distances massively. Always check walking time.
Everything costs more than you expectA pint is £6–8. A coffee is £4–5. Dinner for two without wine is easily £60–80. Budget generously.
Cannot hail taxis from the pavement in most areas — use an appBlack cabs: hail when yellow light is on. Uber: app only. Waving at cars on a pavement does not work here.
The weather will change multiple times in a daySun at 10am, rain at 1pm, sun again at 3pm. Londoners find this completely unremarkable.
Queuing is sacred — never jump a queueJoin at the back, always. Jumping generates quiet but unmistakable hostility. This is not negotiable.
Locals walk across bridges — tourists take the TubeWalking Westminster to Tower Bridge via the South Bank takes an hour and passes everything. Best way to see London.
Locals use the top deck of buses as a sightseeing toolBus 11: Fulham to Liverpool Street via Westminster, Trafalgar Sq, St Paul's. Free with contactless. Best tour in the city.
Locals eat outside whenever the sun appears — regardless of temperatureSun appears → Londoners go outside immediately. Parks fill within minutes. Join them.
Locals avoid Borough Market on Saturday lunchtimeThursday–Friday: quieter traders, space to breathe, same food. Saturday lunchtime is wall-to-wall tourists.
Never call Tower Bridge London BridgeTower Bridge: towers and bascules, iconic. London Bridge: plain, flat, upstream. Mix them up and every Londoner will know you're a tourist.
Never eat at a restaurant with photos on the menu near a tourist sitePhotos on the menu near Big Ben = paying tourist prices for food no Londoner would touch. Walk one street further.
Never stop suddenly in the middle of the pavementLondoners walk fast. Stopping mid-pavement to check a map causes pile-ups and real irritation. Step aside first.
Never ask for directions unless truly lostLondoners will help if truly needed but are busy. Be specific. 'Where is the Tube?' works. 'What's a nice restaurant?' causes polite panic.
The Sky Garden is free — book at skygarden.londonBook 3–4 weeks in advance online. One of the best views in London, completely free.
Sir John Soane's Museum — strange and extraordinaryA Georgian house crammed with antiquities and an Egyptian sarcophagus. Free. 13 Lincoln's Inn Fields. Almost no tourists.
Tate Modern has a free top-floor viewing levelLevel 10 of the Blavatnik Building: free, panoramic Thames views over St Paul's and the City. No ticket.
Postman's Park — a forgotten Victorian memorial gardenA wall of memorial tiles to ordinary people who died saving others. Near EC1A 7BT. Unknown to most tourists.

That was London.

Every stop checked. Every story heard. Hope it was legendary.
